own Olivia writes about her experiences with her and her family making free scrubs for hospitals in March. \xc2\xa0 \xc2\xa0Back in March a good friend of mine sent out a request for home sewn uniform bags. The requirements were: 1. To be able to wash at 60 degrees. 2. To fit an NHS uniform in i.e. scrubs or nurses uniform and doctor\'s coat etc. I live just outside of London in the area of Kingston Upon Thames, Covid 19 was being tackled with full force in London and it\'s surrounding boroughs and this was an NHS Matron, a mother and a friend asking for help from us her peers. I can\'t sew, I\'ve tried many times but aside from anything I just don\'t love it. My family however is made up of keen sewers, knitters, crafters. Using the power of whatsapp I contacted my Mum, aunt\'s and sister who all (very quickly I might add) swung into action. From Norfolk,Suffolk, Bedfordshire and Sussex I started receiving bags down here in Kingston. They were measuring, cutting, pinning, stitching and overlocking everyday. As the demand for them in Kingston slowed down, the demand in my family\'s own counties grew so they began to sew and find local groups to work alongside.My Mum is a little bit like me; she has dabbled in knitting, sewing and she can repair something if she really wants to but she knows it is not her skill and her sewing machine is long gone! However, her friend, Cheryl, definitely was on board to help and as the local demand grew we reached out to others to join in and create beautiful bags for the hardworking, brave members of the community who were very much risking their lives everyday at work. Mum\'s home became a sort of "hub", she and my Dad are both at high risk so having already gone through all her bedding, linen and storage of fabric she would leave a box out for fabric donations. Mum would then cut out the patterns and sort the fabrics for the other members of the team to sew/create the bags. They would then drop them back to her box. Then Mum would wash, iron and wrap them with a ribbon and a handwritten card of encouragement. Then back in a different box outside. I would communicate online with those who needed the bags and arrange pick up times. From March to June the team made, packaged, wrote encouraging notes and provided 355 bags for key workers, completely free of charge. Those who made the bags were Cheryl, Gina,Christine and Heather. The bags went to the following localities: St Elizabeth Hospice, Felixstowe Community Hospital Nurses Team, Crescent Road Dental Practice, Grove Road Pharmacy, Grove Court Care Home, Addenbrookes Hospital, Homerton Paramedics London, Felixstowe Community Physio Team and Ipswich Hospitals following departments; Radiography, Cancer Day Care, Outpatients,Bramford, Kirton and Rushmere Wards Thank you to those who donated material, time, effort and love to these creations. Thank you also to my Mum for swinging into action, and finally, to the wonderful members of the community who needed these bags, thank you for your frontline work.
