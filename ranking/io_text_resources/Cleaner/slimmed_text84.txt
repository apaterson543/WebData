I was afraid again for my colleagues as we learned that a doctor in the United States had committed suicide from the post-traumatic stress of running an emergency room.COVID-19 dominated my life in August, as educators demanded plans for September, but we all knew that there was no plan to ensure safety in classrooms. COVID-19 dominated my life as groups of drunken young adults cycled past me on a mobile bar and I yelled at them to put on masks. COVID-19 dominated my life as I gradually opened up my office for more in-person visits and tried to figure out how to focus again on chronic illnesses and preventative medicine.I was afraid of COVID-19 in September when parents chose to withdraw from public education, forming private pods. I was afraid when testing centres were overwhelmed, and I watched my patients form long lines, standing for hours with sick children in their arms. People with disabilities couldn\xe2\x80\x99t access tests. People started to pay huge sums for private tests. I was afraid when I received a memo from Public Health to say that we should have a \xe2\x80\x9clow threshold for testing,\xe2\x80\x9d reminding us that we cannot distinguish clinically between COVID-19 and other respiratory illnesses. I was even more afraid in the following weeks, as our public health guidelines inexplicably changed and we couldn\xe2\x80\x99t separate politics from sound policy. I was afraid and upset when we were notified that there was a case of COVID-19 in my own child\xe2\x80\x99s classroom.
