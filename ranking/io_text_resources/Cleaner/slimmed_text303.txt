volume shifts and more. CrundenDESIGN BY:Nami SumidaThe COVID-19 pandemic has shocked the global economy and brought sweeping changes to the United States in recent months. The waste and recycling industry\xe2\x80\x99s essential public health role means it has been operating throughout, even as many workers have become ill with the virus. Worker safety resources are available from OSHA, SWANA, NWRA and ISRI. Many companies, including the industry\xe2\x80\x99s largest players, have experienced financial challenges. Some have laid off employees as a result, and public sector service providers could face similar pressures in the future. Smaller service providers that qualify have received funds from the federal Paycheck Protection Program. Staffing limitations, volume shifts and social distancing requirements have caused operational changes across many areas in recent months. Numerous curbside recycling programs were suspended, container redemption requirements were waived in most states with bottle bills and other environmental policies have changed.We\xe2\x80\x99ll be tracking the key developments as this pandemic unfolds for as long as it takes.
