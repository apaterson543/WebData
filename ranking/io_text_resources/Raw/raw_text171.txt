infection and general health; many such messages are far more dangerous to you than to him.</p>\n<p>Over the last few days I have received multiple messages with subject lines related to President Trump\xe2\x80\x99s ongoing battle with the novel coronavirus: \xe2\x80\x9cCheck out this information the whitehouse is not sharing about Trump&#8217;s health,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cThe document Republicans don\xe2\x80\x99t want you to see about Trump\xe2\x80\x99s covid-19,\xe2\x80\x9d and other similarly-titled messages seem to arrive in my mailbox on a regular basis throughout the day.</p>\n<p>While messages with such subject lines are certainly enticing to read \xe2\x80\x93 <strong>do not open them or follow the instructions in them</strong>; many are nothing more than cleverly disguised cyberattack vehicles; in some cases, the aforementioned emails that I received contained a poisoned attachment intended to infect the reader\xe2\x80\x99s computer with malware, and, in other cases, the emails linked to a Google Document, which, rather than containing information about President Trump\xe2\x80\x99s health, contained a link to a potentially malicious website disguised as a document-download link.</p>\n<p>As we have seen on a regular basis for many years, cybercriminals constantly exploit trending news and topics of interest to solicit victims through social engineering attacks; <strong>please get your news from reliable news sources, and do not let President Trump\xe2\x80\x99s misfortune become one of your own</strong>.</p>\n<div style=\'clear:
