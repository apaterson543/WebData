affected. Around one in seven workers in the UK are self-employed \xe2\x80\x93 roughly 15% of the workforce \xe2\x80\x93 and many of them have seen their work dry up from the day lockdown began. Millions of the self-employed have also fallen through the gaps in the government income support schemes and continue to be <a href="https://labourlist.org/2020/09/why-rishi-sunaks-new-support-scheme-is-not-up-to-the-job/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">excluded from financial help</a>. They have been failed by the government.</p>\n<p>Research by the Resolution Foundation shows almost half of self-employed people have lost work due to lack of demand and lockdown restrictions, or for health or caring reasons such as shielding and childcare. Our members have told us similar stories, with 58% experiencing a large fall in income due to the pandemic and many failing to qualify for help from the government\xe2\x80\x99s income support schemes. Like most other self-employed workers, our members are suffering real financial hardship. Many of them feel \xe2\x80\x93 and have been \xe2\x80\x93 forgotten by the government.</p>\n<p>Despite the precarious situation facing the self-employed, who are not entitled to sick pay and other benefits, the government is now <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/tax/news/chancellor-sunak-eyes-national-insurance-hike-return-self-employed/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">thinking about</a> raising National Insurance contributions for self-employed workers as a quid pro quo. Yet what the government fails to understand is that self-employed workers are often at a significant disadvantage to full-time employees. This crisis has exposed this inequality and the precarious situation faced by so many self-employed people. Rather than hiking National Insurance contributions, the government should take a holistic approach to the self-employed workforce, which includes introducing additional and long-term support.</p>\n<p>That\xe2\x80\x99s why we are working with Prospect trade union to launch a new independent inquiry into the future of self-employment. We\xe2\x80\x99ll be assessing the crisis facing the UK\xe2\x80\x99s five million-strong self-employed workforce in the wake of the pandemic and will map out long-term solutions for the government to listen to, learn from and adopt. Backed by Martin Lewis and with commissioners from across the political, academic and industry divide, we\xe2\x80\x99ll be looking at issues such as the future tax regime and safety-net for these workers, their job security and employment rights, and how to improve government understanding of the self-employed and avoid unequal support for different types of workers.</p>\n<p>Our self-employed community has grown significantly over recent years and \xe2\x80\x93 as a result of the impact of the pandemic on our economy \xe2\x80\x93 we will likely see more people seek out new ways of working. As trade unions, we\xe2\x80\x99re proud champions of the self-employed and freelance community across the UK, and we\xe2\x80\x99re here to help and support them.
