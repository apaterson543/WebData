class="sharethis-inline-share-buttons social-right"></div>\n<p class="date">Thursday, October 08, 2020</p>\n<p class="byline"><em>GoLocalProv News Team</em></p>\n<div class="clear">&nbsp;</div>\n<p>\n<div id="entry_image" style="width: 360px" class="right">\n<p class="image-container" style="position:relative;"><a class="fancybox" href="https://www.golocalprov.com/cache/images/cached/cache/images/remote/https_s3.amazonaws.com/media.golocalprov.com/Politics/raimondo_budget_MCCAFFREY_850_850_90.JPG" title="RI Governor Gina Raimondo is asking retired teachers to consider substituting. Photo: GoLocal "><img src="/cache/images/cached/cache/images/remote/https_s3.amazonaws.com/media.golocalprov.com/Politics/raimondo_budget_MCCAFFREY_360_360_90.JPG" alt="" width="360" height="360" /><span class="expand">View Larger +</span></a></p>\n<p class="caption">RI Governor Gina Raimondo is asking retired teachers to consider substituting. Photo: GoLocal </p>\n</div>\nRhode Island Governor Gina Raimondo is asking retired teachers to consider coming back as substitutes -- and health officials, and retired teachers -- have concerns.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;[Schools] need more teachers, particularly those that can support the distance learning options,&rdquo; said Raimondo on Wednesday. &ldquo;I&rsquo;m putting a call out to all [retired] Rhode Island teachers, please consider coming back to be a substitute teacher, and consider&hellip;virtual learning.&rdquo;</p>\n<p>Former Rhode Island Director of Health Dr. Michael Fine said regarding in-person teaching &mdash; which Raimondo continues to push for &mdash; coronavirus mortality risk increases &ldquo;exponentially&rdquo; over the age of 50.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;People&rsquo;s risk rises as their age and weight go up. There&rsquo;s also some association of higher risk with the male gender,&rdquo; said Fine.</p>\n<p>&ldquo;People have to make their own choices,&rdquo; he added. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s worth thinking through the relative degree of community transmission in each different community before an individual makes that decision.&rdquo;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;People under 50 don&rsquo;t appear to be at any higher risk, but the risk goes up every ten years. And this is a risk of death and not of disease,&rdquo; said Fine. &ldquo;For people in their 50s the risk is a couple of deaths for every 10,000 people &mdash; it rises to 10% for people in their 80s.&rdquo;</p>\n<p><strong>Weighing Options - and Past</strong></p>\n<p>&ldquo;We&rsquo;re an age group with a bullseye on our chest,&rdquo; said Diane Bucci, a retired teacher who spent 30 years in the Portsmouth school system.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>Bucci, who serves as a board member on the Rhode Island Retired Teachers Association, said she thinks some retired teachers might consider the option &mdash; or not.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;I know I&rsquo;m not the most internet-savvy,&rdquo; she said, of online learning. &ldquo;I think she&rsquo;ll get some people though. Teachers are generally kind people.&rdquo;</p>\n<p>Bucci, however, took issue with Raimondo&rsquo;s pension reform as part of the Rhode Island Retirement Security Act of 2011, which drastically cut back teachers&#39; defined benefits &mdash; and suspended cost-of-living-adjustments (COLAs) for retirees.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;The way we&rsquo;ve been treated by the Governor, I&rsquo;m not sure how much interest there is in doing this favor,&rdquo; said Bucci.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>Bucci said that she knows firsthand how much of an impact the lack of COLAs has had on retirees.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;Life events have been made very difficult for retirees, whether it be living in aging houses, having health issues, or maybe just that opportunity as grandparents to be a little more generous, without having that benefit of a COLA we were promised,&rdquo; said Bucci.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>&ldquo;So now we&rsquo;re needed &mdash; now we&rsquo;re worth something, literally and figuratively,&rdquo; said Bucci.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>On Wednesday, Raimondo also made an appeal to those who have never taught before, to consider substituting.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>"Even if you&rsquo;ve never been a teacher, RIDE has
