\n</figure></div>\n                                    \n                                \n                                \n    <h2 class="ArticlePage-subheadline">The alcohol licenses of nine establishments were suspended after state officials said they were not complying with coronavirus regulations.</h2>\n\n\n                            \n\n                            \n\n                            <div class="ArticlePage-articleContainer">\n                                \n                                    <div class="ArticlePage-articleBody">\n                                        <p>Drinks can flow at establishments where alcohol licenses were pulled this summer when the state cracked down on violations of coronavirus rules.</p><p>Nine alcohol license suspensions issued by the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation between June 22 and Aug. 10 have been lifted, department spokesman Patrick Fargason said in an email Tuesday.</p><p>\xe2\x80\x9cAs of Friday, October 2, 2020, all nine (emergency suspension order) cases have been resolved through a final order; therefore, the nine ESOs that were entered have been vacated, and the suspensions have been lifted from each license,\xe2\x80\x9d Fargason said.</p><p>Gov. Ron DeSantis stopped bars and nightclubs from serving alcohol for on-site consumption as part of an emergency order on March 20 to try to help stop the spread of the virus. The order was lifted on June 5 in all but South Florida, which faced the strongest outbreaks of the virus.</p><p>At the time, the state limited bars to 50 percent indoor occupancy and required patrons to be seated at tables when served alcohol.</p><p>The suspensions -- some of which drew heavy publicity -- started with The Knights Pub in Orlando on June 22. The order directed at the Orlando pub noted a disregard for \xe2\x80\x9cthe well-known dangers of COVID-19\xe2\x80\x9d and that the business was \xe2\x80\x9clikely\xe2\x80\x9d to continue the \xe2\x80\x9charmful business practices and behavior.\xe2\x80\x9d</p><p>The pub\xe2\x80\x99s suspension was followed by similar actions against Bajas Beachclub in Tallahassee, Le Palace Otown in Orlando, Show-N-Tail in Panama City, Pegasus Lounge &amp; Package in Tampa, Mathers, Shots and Joysticks in Orlando, Rusty\xe2\x80\x99s Raw Bar and Grill in Estero, Pockets Pool &amp; Pub in Tallahassee and The George and Dragon British Tavern in Cocoa Village.</p><p>On June 26, Department of Business and Professional Regulation Secretary Halsey Beshears re-imposed a ban on onsite consumption of alcohol at bars because he said non-compliance with coronavirus safety guidelines was considered too widespread to enforce. The ban, however, included an exception for establishments that served food.</p><p>Beshears announced Sept. 10 that he was rescinding the ban.</p><p>Taps reopened at The Knights Pub on Sept. 14, with the business noting on Facebook it was operating under federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention guidelines and requiring people to reserve a table, as \xe2\x80\x9cNo walk-ups will be admitted entry!\xe2\x80\x9d</p><p>The announcement was welcomed by most, although one Facebook user retorted, \xe2\x80\x9cI can buy beer at the store and not have to wear a mask or have my personal information collected. Good luck with the new Nazi rules, I\'m sure there will be a few alcoholics to keep you somewhat afloat financially.\xe2\x80\x9d</p><p>Some of the other businesses hit by the suspension had their licenses reinstated earlier.</p><p>The owners of The George and Dragon British Tavern on Aug. 28 offered online praise to their attorney for helping get the establishment reopened after its alcohol license was suspended on July 14. The owners also noted additional cleaning was going to be conducted before the doors were unlocked to the public.</p><p>An employee of Pegasus Lounge &amp; Package on Monday noted the business had been serving alcohol for almost a month. At Le Palace Otown, an employee said Monday that service restarted \xe2\x80\x9ca long time ago.\xe2\x80\x9d</p><p>A legal challenge by Show-N-Tail against the state at the 1st District Court of Appeal was voluntarily dismissed Monday. Bajas Beachclub on its Facebook page announced a grand reopening last Saturday.<br></p>\n                                    </div>\n                                \n                                \n                            </div>\n                        </article>\n\n                        \n\n<div class="ArticlePage-tags">\n<h2 class="ArticlePage-tags-heading">Tags</h2>\n    <div class="ArticlePage-tags-list"><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/economy-business"   >Economy / Business</a><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/tags/coronavirus"   >Coronavirus</a><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/tags/covid-19"   >COVID-19</a><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/tags/bars"   >bars</a><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/tags/department-of-business-and-professional-regulation"   >Department of Business and Professional Regulation</a><a class="Link"  href="https://wusfnews.wusf.usf.edu/tags/gov-ron-desantis"   >Gov.
