target="_blank">Brazil: Struggling with life after COVID-19</a></b></h2><p><b>Many people who contracted the coronavirus disease \nstruggle with long-term health issues. Maria Alzenir, a Sao Paulo local,\n got infected in July but is still dealing with a chronic shortness of \nbreath and muscle pains.</b></p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="529" data-orig-width="940"><img src="https://64.media.tumblr.com/bb9bf0629901474b7f4bc99074970784/e4e47cb07be14ef7-38/s500x750/a2689dd0e30d274c86f17b4e1603ae0d7586824b.png" data-orig-height="529" data-orig-width="940" width="500" height="281" alt="image" /></figure><p>Maria Alzenir Lima was released from hospital one month ago. The \n53-year-old Sao Paulo local has officially recovered from her COVID-19 \ninfection, yet still struggles with a <a href="https://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.dw.com%2Fen%2Fcoronavirus-symptoms%2Fa-52233885&amp;t=MDdhOWRkM2U3NDU3MmJiOTEzMGQwMDA4YzcyNDA4YTg0YWNjYmJiNixJTjRwV3RlSA%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3A5uGe0poqKLmZzqDGpAMprQ&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fallthebrazilianpolitics.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F631341938788958208%2Fbrazil-struggling-with-life-after-covid-19-many&amp;m=1&amp;ts=1602796983" target="_blank">persistent cough and shortness of breath.</a> Getting around is a serious challenge. And after all this time, she still needs supplemental oxygen. </p><p>Alzenir\n &mdash; a nurse by training &mdash; spent 40 days in hospital. Her husband, who \ncontracted SARS-CoV-2 as well, was hospitalized one day after her. He \ndied on August 4 from heart and kidney complications brought about by \nthe illness.</p><p>&ldquo;I still cannot believe it. When I was hospitalized,\n my husband was still doing fine,&rdquo; says Alzenir. &ldquo;I find it hard to \ngrasp that he will not be at home when I go back.&rdquo; Alzenir has not yet \nreturned to her own house but has been living with her daughter Barbara \nLima since being discharged, as she requires round-the-clock care.</p><p>Last week, when the global coronavirus death toll passed 1 million, with\n 140,000 of those deaths recorded in Brazil alone, Alzenir did her \n10th physiotherapy session at her daughter&rsquo;s home to deal with the \nconsequences of her long hospital stay. Alzenir&rsquo;s leg muscles have grown\n weak and walking is difficult for her. She says that immediately after \nbeing discharged, she could not even stand upright. Now, at least, she \ncan walk a few steps, she says.</p><p>A study by South Korea&rsquo;s Disease Control and Prevention Agency found \nthat nine out of 10 COVID-19 patients suffer from long-term issues like \nfatigue or a loss of smell and taste after recovering from the illness. \nSome 26% of those who participated in the study reported suffering from \nchronic fatigue, making it the most common long-term symptom. Nature,\n a weekly science journal, cites a further study revealing neurological \ndamage caused by the virus. And according to a Japanese study, COVID-19 \nmay <a href="https://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.dw.com%2Fen%2Fcoronavirus-brain-damage%2Fa-54111054&amp;t=ZWJkZjIwOWIwODBlNmMyZThiZjU5MDY1YmE0OWUwZTQxZDVlYWQ0OSxJTjRwV3RlSA%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3A5uGe0poqKLmZzqDGpAMprQ&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fallthebrazilianpolitics.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F631341938788958208%2Fbrazil-struggling-with-life-after-covid-19-many&amp;m=1&amp;ts=1602796983" target="_blank">also damage brain tissue.</a></p><p><a href="https://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.dw.com%2Fen%2Fcovid-19-brazil%2Fa-55179978&amp;t=NTNlMjc0YjVkMTI2NWQ5YWRhMmEyMDY2MTlmNjZhNDU5ODg3YTU0ZSxJTjRwV3RlSA%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3A5uGe0poqKLmZzqDGpAMprQ&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fallthebrazilianpolitics.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F631341938788958208%2Fbrazil-struggling-with-life-after-covid-19-many&amp;m=1&amp;ts=1602796983" target="_blank"><b>Continue reading.</b></a><br /></p>\r\n    \r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n    <footer class="post-info">\r\n        \r\n
