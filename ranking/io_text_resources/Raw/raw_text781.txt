nationwide drive to boost the ailing economy ahead of a key party congress in January, state media reported Tuesday.<br />\n<span id="more-30630"></span></p>\n<p>The decision was made during a meeting of the ruling Workers&#8217; Party and comes as the coronavirus pandemic and recent floods have added yet more pressure on the isolated country&#8217;s flagging economy.</p>\n<p>Mandatory mass mobilization drives, featuring extra-long work hours and additional duties, are commonplace in North Korea in the lead-up to major events.</p>\n<p>The efforts have usually been dubbed &#8220;Battles&#8221; &#8212; the North often embraces militaristic terminology and notions of struggle &#8212; but while the official KCNA news agency described it as such in Korean, in its English version it used the more diplomatic term &#8220;campaign&#8221;.</p>\n<p>&#8220;We have performed historical feats with our costly efforts, boldly overcoming unprecedentedly grave trials and difficulties this year, but we should not rest on our laurels,&#8221; it said.</p>\n<p>&#8220;We still face the challenges that cannot be overlooked and there are many goals we have to attain within this year.&#8221;</p>\n<p>Participation in the exhausting &#8220;battles&#8221; is rigorously monitored and used as a measure of loyalty to the regime, with past campaigns denounced by rights groups as exercises in forced labour.</p>\n<p>North Korea&#8217;s ruling party is due to lay out a new economic plan at its January congress &#8212; the first such meeting in five years.</p>\n<p>The North has suffered from chronic economic mismanagement and a previous plan was quietly scrapped earlier this year, with a party meeting in August concluding that &#8220;goals for improving the national economy have been seriously delayed&#8221;.</p>\n<p>It has also been hit by international sanctions imposed in response to its ballistic missile and nuclear programmes, which have made rapid progress under Kim&#8217;s leadership.</p>\n<p>Analysts expect Pyongyang to showcase the latest developments in those programmes on Saturday, at a grand event to mark the 75th anniversary of the country&#8217;s ruling party.
