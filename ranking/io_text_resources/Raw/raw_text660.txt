RESIDENTS&nbsp; have been urged to remain vigilant as the number of <a href="/news/coronavirus/" class="section-link">coronavirus</a> cases across Hampshire continues to rise.\n</p>\n<p>\n  A total of 258 Covid-19 cases were recorded across the Hampshire County Council area in the seven days up to October 5.\n</p>\n<div id="subscription-content">\n<p>\n  This brings the total of cases in the area to 6,251.\n</p>\n<p>\n  It comes as at the end of September the total number of cases across the county was&nbsp;6,006.\n</p>\n<p>\n  Civic chiefs at Hampshire County Council said the numbers are below the national average but the increase is "concerning".\n</p><div id="inArticleAd-1" class="advert-container"><div id="DFP_in_article_mpu"></div></div>\n<p>\n  The number of reported cases has also gone up in <a href="/news/district/southampton/" class="section-link">Southampton</a> in the past week.\n</p>\n<p>\n  According to data published by Southampton City Council, 94 new cases were recorded in the city last week.\n</p>\n<p>\n  This is 59 more cases than the week before.\n</p>\n<p>\n  Simon Bryant, director of public <a href="/news/health/" class="section-link">health</a> at the county council, said the infection rate in the county stands at 18.5x100,000 population.\n</p>\n<p>\n  He said this is below the South East average of 24.2x100,000 and the national average of 80.5x100,000.\n</p><div id="piano-container-inside-article" style="margin-bottom: 20px; clear: both;"></div>\n<p>\n  But talking to councillors&nbsp;at the meeting of the Hampshire Local Outbreak Engagement Board he said: "We are seeing&nbsp;an increase in cases week on week and we are below the national rate but that is concerning and we are monitoring that very carefully to take any action that is required."\n</p>\n<p>\n  A document published by the authority also revealed the Rushmoor district is the only one&nbsp;in Hampshire to have an infection&nbsp;rate higher than the national average.\n</p>\n<p>\n  Mr Byrant said that this is due to a more diverse community&nbsp;in the area and he said that work&nbsp; is ongoing to ensure that messages are delivered to every member of the community.\n</p>\n<p>\n  Talking about the increase in cases across Hampshire he added: "We are seeing general increases in cases and an increased number of cases in <a href="/news/education/" class="section-link">education</a> settings, not outbreaks but cases. We are seeing single cases in schools not outbreaks."\n</p>\n<p>\n  Meanwhile, last week the infection rate in Southampton was at 14.24x100,000.\n</p>\n<p>\n  The total of cases in the city since the start of the pandemic has now risen to 1,227.\n</p><div id="inArticleAd-2">\n    <div id="taboola-nq-mid-article" data-replaces="DFP_in_article_2"></div>\n    <script type="text/javascript">\n    window._taboola = window._taboola || [];\n    if (whiteList !== true) {\n        if (isMobile()) {\n            midArticleTaboolaOptions = {\n                mode: \'thumbnails-mid-mob\',\n                container: \'taboola-nq-mid-article\',\n                placement: \'NQ Mid Article Mobile\',\n                target_type: \'mix\'\n            }\n        } else {\n            midArticleTaboolaOptions = {\n                mode: \'thumbnails-mid-desk\',\n                container: \'taboola-nq-mid-article\',\n                placement: \'NQ Mid Article Desktop\',\n                target_type: \'mix\'\n            }\n        }\n        _taboola.push(midArticleTaboolaOptions);\n    }\n    </script></div>\n<p>\n  Dr Debbie Chase, director of public health at Southampton City Council, said the increase in cases was expected.\n</p>\n<p>\n  She added: "Southampton&rsquo;s rates reflect a growing number of positive cases being identified through widespread testing, as well as small numbers of linked cases that are classed as outbreaks.
