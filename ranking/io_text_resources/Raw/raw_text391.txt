By breaking many of our daily rituals, it has also disconnected us\nfrom the habits that lead us to interact with others.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>While self-isolation makes it more difficult to stay in touch\nwith people, the internet is a powerful tool to mitigate this problem.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>Even something as simple as a phone call to a friend or\nfamily member can be great for helping you feel less alone. Keep in mind that\nyou\xe2\x80\x99re not just protecting your mental health: you\xe2\x80\x99re protecting theirs by\nchecking in on them.</p>\n\n\n\n<h4> Disconnecting From The News </h4>\n\n\n\n<p>While the internet is great for staying in touch with\nothers, both it and cable news can also give us access to a wealth of\ninformation we don\xe2\x80\x99t need.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>It\xe2\x80\x99s important to understand the basic precautions you can\ntake to protect yourself and others from the coronavirus: maintaining at least\nsix feet of distance from others, regularly washing your hands for at least 20\nseconds, not touching your face, and so on.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>At the same time, you do not need minute-by-minute updates describing\nthe current spread of the coronavirus. In this situation, it\xe2\x80\x99s best to focus on\nwhat you can control: your own response to the outbreak.</p>\n\n\n\n<h4>Don\xe2\x80\x99t Put Pressure On Yourself</h4>\n\n\n\n<p>You may feel that staying at home all day obligates you to\nachieve some goal you\xe2\x80\x99ve been thinking about. Reading a classic work of\nliterature or trying to pick up a new skill can be a great way to pass the time\nand feel good, but you shouldn\xe2\x80\x99t let it stress you out.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>Being forced to stay at home is making many people feel anxious\nand depressed. If you don\xe2\x80\x99t feel ready to conquer a big task right now, that\xe2\x80\x99s\ncompletely understandable. You may instead choose to focus on smaller daily\nthings that make you feel better: showering, cleaning up the house, and so on.</p>\n\n\n\n<h4>Struggling with Relapse</h4>\n\n\n\n<p>Recovering from addiction is a lifelong process. This makes\nstressful times particularly difficult for those struggling with addiction.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>There are a few basic steps you can take to help during this\ntime: making sure the substance(s) in question aren\xe2\x80\x99t present in your\nhousehold, as well as speaking with loved ones who can help hold you\naccountable.</p>\n\n\n\n<p>If you have relapsed, please get help.
