<div class="nhsuk-grid-column-two-thirds">\n\n      \n  \n\n  \n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="">\n  \n    <p><strong>Germs can be spread from person to person or by touching unclean equipment or surfaces.</strong></p>\n<p>To stop the spread of germs, focus your efforts on cleaning areas in the house where germs are more likely to spread, such as the kitchen and toilet.</p>\n<p>Use either soap and hot water to rinse the germs away, or a disinfectant to kill them. </p>\n<p>Make sure\xc2\xa0you dry surfaces\xc2\xa0such as\xc2\xa0worktops and chopping boards thoroughly\xc2\xa0after cleaning.\xc2\xa0Dampness helps any remaining germs to survive and, if there\'s enough water, multiply.</p>\n<p>You should clean germ hotspots on a regular basis after use,\xc2\xa0rather than the customary once-a-week deep clean.\xc2\xa0 </p>\n<p>Cleaning aids, such as cloths or mops, must be germ-free or they\'ll spread germs to other surfaces. </p>\n<p>Here are some general cleaning tips to help prevent the spread of germs in your home:</p>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="cloths-and-sponges">\n  \n    <h2>Cloths and sponges</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>use disposable cloths or paper towels when possible </li>\n<li>reusable cloths should be\xc2\xa0disinfected or washed at 60C (140F) after each use </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="washing-up-brushes">\n  \n    <h2>Washing-up brushes</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>wash brushes in a dishwasher regularly or clean with detergent and warm water after each use </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="mops-and-buckets">\n  \n    <h2>Mops and buckets</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>use two buckets for mopping \xe2\x80\x93 one for detergent and the other for rinsing </li>\n<li>mops and buckets should be cleaned and dried after each use </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="toilets">\n  \n    <h2>Toilets</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>keep the U-bend and\xc2\xa0toilet bowl clean by flushing after each use </li>\n<li>use a toilet cleaner and brush every few days </li>\n<li>limescale should be regularly removed using a descaling product </li>\n<li>keep the\xc2\xa0toilet seat, handle and rim clean by using a disinfectant </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="baths-and-sinks">\n  \n    <h2>Baths and sinks</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>clean baths and sinks frequently, if they\'re\xc2\xa0used regularly </li>\n<li>use disinfectant if they\'ve been used by someone who\'s ill </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="showers">\n  \n    <h2>Showers</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>clean shower trays frequently, if used regularly </li>\n<li>if a shower hasn\'t been used for a while, let it run with hot water before using it </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="tiles-and-shower-curtains">\n  \n    <h2>Tiles and shower curtains</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>keep tiles and grout in good condition </li>\n<li>clean shower curtains frequently </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="kitchen">\n  \n    <h2>Kitchen</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>ensure food-preparation surfaces are clean before use </li>\n<li>use separate chopping boards for uncooked food \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0such as\xc2\xa0raw meat \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0and food that doesn\'t need cooking, like salad leaves </li>\n<li>wash and dry your hands after handling foods such as raw meat </li>\n<li>clean surfaces immediately after use </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="floors">\n  \n    <h2>Floors</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>clean floors regularly\xc2\xa0with warm water and detergent to remove visible dirt </li>\n<li>if soiled with vomit,\xc2\xa0urine or poo, the floor should be cleaned using a disposable cloth and warm water, then disinfected \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0make sure the floor is dry before allowing children on it </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="carpet-and-soft-furnishings">\n  \n    <h2>Carpet and soft furnishings</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>steam cleaning is effective against germs on carpets and furnishings </li>\n<li>curtains can be cleaned by laundering or disinfected by steam cleaning </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="pets-and-other-animals">\n  \n    <h2>Pets and other animals</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>keep pet food separate from human food </li>\n<li>always wash your hands after touching animals and their food, toys, cages and litter trays </li>\n<li>dishes, utensils and tin openers used for pet food should be stored separately </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="toys">\n  \n    <h2>Toys</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>clean hard or plastic toys by washing them and\xc2\xa0putting them away\xc2\xa0once they\'re clean and dry </li>\n<li>some soft toys can be cleaned in the washing machine </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="laundry">\n  \n    <h2>Laundry</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>wash your hands after handling dirty laundry </li>\n<li>all underwear, towels and household linen should be washed at 60C (140F) or at 40C (104F) with a bleach-based laundry product to prevent\xc2\xa0germs from spreading\xc2\xa0 </li>\n<li>don\'t leave laundry in the washing machine \xe2\x80\x93\xc2\xa0any remaining germs can multiply rapidly </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n    \n\n<section id="waste-disposal">\n  \n    <h2>Waste disposal</h2>\n<ul>\n<li>foot-operated bins are more hygienic because they reduce the risk of\xc2\xa0getting germs onto your hands\xc2\xa0from touching the bin lid </li>\n<li>always wash your hands after handling waste material </li>\n<li>throw rubbish away carefully to avoid attracting vermin and insects </li>\n</ul>\n\n  \n</section>\n\n  \n\n  <div class="nhsuk-review-date">\n    <p
