popularising the GPU technology about two decades ago, Nvidia Corporation (<a href="https://news.alphastreet.com/symbol/nvda/">NASDAQ: NVDA</a>) has dominated the market for that fast-growing microprocessor category so far. Of late, the chipmaker has been facing competition from Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. However, they are poles apart when it comes to the price -\xe2\x80\x93 currently trading slightly above $85, AMD is much more affordable. <strong>But </strong>u<strong>nlike Nvidia, AMD seems to have peaked after the recent rally and the relatively high valuation makes it less attractive.</strong> Experts warn that the value might drop in mid-single-digit percentages in the coming months. In contrast, Nvidia has outperformed both AMD and the <a href="https://news.alphastreet.com/micron-mu-stock-suffers-despite-strong-q4-numbers-as-chipmaker-dumps-huawei/">semiconductor industry</a> so far this year.</p>\n\n\n\n<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Also read: <a href="https://news.alphastreet.com/advanced-micro-devices-inc-amd-q2-2020-earnings-call-transcript/">AMD Q2 2020 earnings call transcript</a></span></strong></p>\n\n\n\n<p>After chasing semiconductor giant <a href="https://news.alphastreet.com/intel-corporation-nasdaq-intc-reports-q2-2020-earnings-results/">Intel (INTC)</a> for a long time, AMD is currently enjoying an edge over its bigger rival by shipping the high-end 7nm processors that are a notch above their Intel equivalent. Though AMD was initially successful in gaining market share in GPU &#8212; an area considered to be Nvidia&#8217;s core competency &#8212; the latest data point to a climbdown from the prior-year levels.</p>\n\n\n\n<h2>Why Nvidia</h2>\n\n\n\n<p><strong>The primary factor that gives Nvidia an advantage over others is its strong fundamentals and liquidity</strong> \xe2\x80\x93 operating cash flow was $1.6 billion at the end of the July-quarter.
