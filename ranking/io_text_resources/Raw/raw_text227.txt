                <div class="align-justify details_content"><p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\t<a href="https://www.reuters.com/" target="_blank"><strong>REUTERS</strong></a></p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tItaly on Wednesday made it mandatory to wear face masks outdoors nationwide in an effort to curb the spread of the coronavirus, as new infections jumped to the highest daily tally since April.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tThe decree was approved at a cabinet meeting after a steady increase in cases over the last two months. It will probably be effective from Thursday, a government source said.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tSeveral Italian regions including Lazio, around the capital Rome, had already made Britain.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tItaly was the first country in Europe to be hard-hit by COVID-19 and has the second highest death toll in the continent after Britain, with 36,061 COVID-related deaths remain far lower than during the first wave of Italy&rsquo;s epidemic six months ago.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tSome 31 people died of the virus on Wednesday, the Health Ministry said, while April 24, the last day with more than 3,000 cases, saw 420 deaths.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tThe cabinet on Wednesday also approved a decree to extend the COVID-19 state of emergency to Jan. 31.</p>\r\n<p style="text-align: justify;">\r\n\tThe state of emergency, originally due to expire in mid-October, gives greater powers to central
