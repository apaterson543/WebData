The problem was that the park was closed under the island\xe2\x80\x99s pandemic restrictions. </p><p>  When the officer approached, one of the men explained that he had traveled to the island to work directly with Hawaii Gov. David Ige (D) on coronavirus prevention. The officer was unmoved, citing the men for breaking the emergency orders. </p><p>  It\xe2\x80\x99s unclear, based on documents reviewed by The Washington Post, whether the officer knew that one of the men cited that day is <a href="https://www.hhs.gov/about/leadership/jerome-adams/index.html">the \xe2\x80\x9cNation\xe2\x80\x99s Doctor.\xe2\x80\x9d</a> </p><p>  U.S. Surgeon General Jerome Adams now faces a remote court date on Oct. 21 to answer charges that he violated the emergency orders by entering the park, a misdemeanor offense that could result in up to a $5,000 fine, up to a year in jail or both, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/us-surgeon-general-cited-for-being-in-closed-hawaii-park/2020/10/06/9362c79c-0833-11eb-8719-0df159d14794_story.html">the Associated Press reported</a>. </p><p>  Adams\xe2\x80\x99s office didn\xe2\x80\x99t immediately return a message seeking comment; his spokeswoman declined to discuss the case with the AP. An attorney representing Adams said that the surgeon general didn\xe2\x80\x99t realize the park was closed. </p><p>  \xe2\x80\x9cDuring his visit to Oahu, the surgeon general was cited for accidentally violating the mayor\xe2\x80\x99s emergency order, due to his misunderstanding of the law,\xe2\x80\x9d Lex Smith, an attorney in Honolulu, said in a statement to the AP. \xe2\x80\x9cHe has not asked for, nor has he received, any special treatment in connection with this citation, and will respond to it appropriately.\xe2\x80\x9d </p><p>  The case comes to light as President Trump and his administration are assailed with charges that <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/white-house-signals-stronger-coronavirus-precautions-but-trump-continues-to-resist/2020/10/06/05cd0be4-07e9-11eb-859b-f9c27abe638d_story.html">they haven\xe2\x80\x99t taken coronavirus restrictions seriously</a>, a long-standing criticism given new currency by an outbreak that has now led to <a href="https://twitter.com/kylegriffin1/status/1313690513644552194">at least 18 coronavirus infections in the White House</a>, including Trump and the first lady. </p><p>  But Adams may be among the least likely Trump administration officials to face a criminal case for violating pandemic restrictions. A self-described \xe2\x80\x9cnice person,\xe2\x80\x9d Adams is so rule-obsessed that he refused to cross the street on a mostly abandoned Mall until a red light changed when The Post\xe2\x80\x99s Manuel Roig-Franzia <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/surgeon-general-jerome-adams-may-be-the-nicest-guy-in-the-trump-administration-but-is-that-what-america-needs-right-now/2020/07/11/39529cec-a1c1-11ea-9590-1858a893bd59_story.html">profiled him in July</a>. </p>Surgeon General Jerome Adams may be the nicest guy in the Trump administration. But is that what America needs right now?<p>  The 46-year-old anesthesiologist has faced strong criticism for his leadership since the beginning of the pandemic, especially <a href="https://twitter.com/jeromeadamsmd/status/1223586401280757762?lang=en">for a poem</a> he tweeted in February suggesting that the flu was a more pressing risk than the coronavirus, which has now killed more <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/national/coronavirus-us-cases-deaths/?itid=hp_pandemic-guide-box">than 210,000 Americans</a>. His national presence has mostly disappeared since an April appearance where he was accused of using racially insensitive language in urging minority groups to take the virus more seriously. </p><p>  But Adams has also become a strong voice in urging Americans to wear masks. He has recently traveled to coronavirus hotspots nationwide, urging the public to follow health and safety guidelines. </p><p>  That\xe2\x80\x99s why he was in Hawaii in August. The state, which has now recorded nearly 13,000 cases and 160 deaths as of early Wednesday, was seeing a surge in new cases at the time. To stem the tide, Honolulu Mayor Kirk Caldwell (D) issued emergency orders that, among other measures, <a href="https://www.staradvertiser.com/2020/09/08/breaking-news/most-oahu-beaches-parks-hiking-trail-to-reopen-thursday-for-solo-use-only/">closed all public parks</a>. </p><p>  Adams and his cohorts were violating that order, police said, when they were spotted in Kualoa on Aug. 23. The officer watched them strolling around the park and taking photos, and noted that Adams pulled a mask back on before returning to his vehicle. The surgeon general told the officer at the time that he didn\xe2\x80\x99t know the parks were closed, according to his citation. </p><p>  An aide to Adams, Dennis Anderson-Villaluz, was also cited in the park, the AP reported. </p><p>  Three days after his citation, Adams stood next to the mayor whose emergency order he had allegedly violated to urge Hawaiians to follow the rules. </p><p>  \xe2\x80\x9cWe\xe2\x80\x99re seeing cases continue to go up, we\xe2\x80\x99re seeing positivity continue to go up,\xe2\x80\x9d Adams <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-3P7LZSnGj0">said at an
