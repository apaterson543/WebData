This airborne transmission is likely the most prevalent path for Covid-19 infections, they say, adding that health officials must communicate this risk more clearly.</p><p id="2f14" class="ij ik eo il b fn im in io fq ip iq ir is it iu iv iw ix iy iz ja jb jc jd je eh fl">\xe2\x80\x9cThe reality is that airborne t<span id="rmm">r</span>ansmission is the main way that transmission happens at close range with prolonged contact,\xe2\x80\x9d says one of the letter\xe2\x80\x99s co-authors, Donald Milton, MD, an environmental health professor at the University of Maryland and an expert on airborne infections. \xe2\x80\x9cYou can\xe2\x80\x99t take your mask off at six feet.\xe2\x80\x9d</p><p id="483d" class="ij ik eo il b fn im in io fq ip iq ir is it iu iv iw ix iy iz ja jb jc jd je eh fl">The indoor superspreader risk has been <a href="https://elemental.medium.com/the-science-of-superspreaders-2ceed2b68834" class="dc jo" rel="noopener">known for months</a> and <a href="https://twitter.com/linseymarr/status/1235640400054046724" class="dc jo" rel="noopener nofollow">communicated frequently by scientists</a> since the spring.
