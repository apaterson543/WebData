Several other major brands including <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/guide/princess-cruises-guide/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Princess Cruises</a>, Holland America and <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/guide/carnival-cruise-line-guide/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Carnival Cruise Line</a> also have canceled all or most November sailings and <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/news/more-carnival-cruises-canceled/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">even some December sailings</a>.</p>\n<p><strong>For more cruise news, reviews and tips, sign up for TPG&rsquo;s <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/cruise-newsletter/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">new cruise newsletter.</a></strong></p>\n<p>Until Tuesday, Royal Caribbean, Celebrity and MSC Cruises only had canceled a significant portion of their sailings through the end of October, giving cruise fans hope that November could be the month that cruising finally starts to resume in earnest.</p>\n<p>A few lines such as MSC Cruises have <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/guide/cruise-lines-restart-service-list/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">restarted limited sailings in Europe</a> and a few other places in recent months, but most cruising has been on hold since health authorities declared COVID-19 a pandemic in March. Royal Caribbean, Celebrity, Norwegian, Princess, Holland America and Carnival haven&rsquo;t operated a single departure over the past seven months.</p>\n<p>The result of the new cancellations on Tuesday is that there now are no major cruise brands catering to North Americans that haven&rsquo;t canceled all or most of their sailings through the end of November.</p>\n<p><strong>Related: <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/guide/points-and-miles-for-cruise-vacation/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">How to book a cruise with points and miles</a>&nbsp;</strong></p>\n<figure id="attachment_1001654" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1001654" style="width: 2560px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img class="wp-image-1001654 size-full lazyautosizes lazyload lazyload blur-up" src="https://thepointsguy.global.ssl.fastly.net/us/originals/2020/09/1530649042_MA18-AerialsFL043-scaled.jpg?width=25" sizes="699px" alt="Mariner of the Seas" width="2560" data-sizes="auto" data-srcset="https://thepointsguy.global.ssl.fastly.net/us/originals/2020/09/1530649042_MA18-AerialsFL043-scaled.jpg?width=700&amp;dpr=1 1x,https://thepointsguy.global.ssl.fastly.net/us/originals/2020/09/1530649042_MA18-AerialsFL043-scaled.jpg?width=700&amp;dpr=2 2x"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-1001654" class="wp-caption-text">Royal Caribbean has canceled nearly all sailings through Nov. 30. (Photo courtesy of Royal Caribbean)</figcaption></figure>\n<p>The new cancellations came just hours after the top executive of <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/news/royal-caribbean-cruises-name-change/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Royal Caribbean Group</a>, the parent company of Royal Caribbean and Celebrity, said he was highly optimistic that cruising in North America would <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/news/caribbean-cruise-comeback-december/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">resume by the end of the year</a>. The comments suggest the company is now targeting a December restart to some cruising out of U.S. ports.</p>\n<p>In making its cancellation announcement late Tuesday, Royal Caribbean said it was canceling all November sailings except for departures out of Hong Kong.</p>\n<p>Celebrity canceled all November departures worldwide. It also canceled all sailings in Australia and Asia scheduled for the coming winter season.</p>\n<p>A third brand owned by Royal Caribbean Group, Azamara, late Tuesday said it was canceling all sailings through at least mid-March. The line now plans to resume service starting on March 20, 2021, with a single ship, Azamara Quest. The line&rsquo;s two other vessels, Azamara Journey and Azamara Pursuit, won&rsquo;t resume sailings until April 9 and May 9, respectively, the line said.</p>\n<p><strong>Related: <a href="https://thepointsguy.com/news/cruise-line-buffets-may-never-return/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">One cruise line considers doing away with the buffet forever</a></strong></p>\n<p>MSC Cruises canceled most sailings through the end of November, except for voyages on two vessels that have resumed operations in the Mediterranean.</p>\n<p>Also canceling more sailings on Tuesday was Seabourn, a small luxury line. The five-ship brand canceled all remaining departures in 2020 as well as a significant number of voyages in the first half of 2021. All but one of the line&rsquo;s ships now aren&rsquo;t scheduled to restart sailing until April or May.</p>\n<p><strong>Planning a cruise?
