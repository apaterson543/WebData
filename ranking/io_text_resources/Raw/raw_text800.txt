Two constitutional amendments \xe2\x80\x93 including a proposal to turn the Public Regulation Commission into an appointed body \xe2\x80\x93 and bond issues are also on the ballot.</p><p>Absentee balloting is off to a strong start. More than 321,000 voters in New Mexico have already requested absentee ballots \xe2\x80\x93 about 24% of registered voters.</p><p>Democrats make up a disproportionate share of the requests. They comprise about 62% of the requests so far, followed by Republicans at 21%. Libertarians, independents and others make up the rest.</p><p>Democrats make up 45% of all registered voters in New Mexico. About 31% of the state&#8217;s registered voters are Republican.</p><p>Only people who have requested an absentee ballot will get one in the mail. The deadline to apply is Oct. 20.</p><p>Secretary of State Maggie Toulouse Oliver said this week that she and other election officials are preparing for an unprecedented influx of absentee ballots as voters opt for the safety of their homes during the coronavirus pandemic.</p><p>Her office has established a voter information portal \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="http://nmvote.org">nmvote.org</a> \xe2\x80\x93 where voters should be able to track their absentee ballot the way they do an Amazon package, with notations on whether their application has been accepted and when the ballot is mailed.</p><p>The Secretary of State Office&#8217;s website, which includes the online voter registration portal, was down for part of the day Tuesday.</p><p>A spokesman for the office said the cause of the outage, which was quickly fixed, was still being investigated.</p><p>Absentee ballots helped push turnout in New Mexico&#8217;s primary election to historic levels. The raw number of ballots cast in the June 2 election \xe2\x80\x93 about 418,000 \xe2\x80\x93 was a record, and the percentage of registered voters who participated \xe2\x80\x93 about 42% \xe2\x80\x93 was the most in at least 20 years.</p><p>&#8220;Elections this year have been in the spotlight probably in a way they never have before,&#8221; Toulouse Oliver said.</p><p>L\xc3\xb3pez Askin \xe2\x80\x93 whose county lies in the 2nd Congressional District, one of the most closely watched House races in the country \xe2\x80\x93 said her staff has seen an explosion of interest in absentee voting for the general election.</p><p>Four years ago, just 3,456 voters cast absentee ballots in Do\xc3\xb1a Ana County. But more than 26,000 voters had already requested absentee ballots through Tuesday morning.</p><p>Manes, in Lea County, and Stover, in Bernalillo County, reported similar increases.</p><p>Voters who have requested an absentee ballot \xe2\x80\x93 but not received it \xe2\x80\x93 can still vote in person. But they will have to sign an affidavit pledging not to vote absentee if the ballot arrives.</p><p>L\xc3\xb3pez Askin said county clerks can track anyone who tries to vote absentee after casting a ballot in person.
