for many businesses across Scotland.</p>\n<p>Nicola Sturgeon has announced all licensed premises will be prohibited from serving alcohol indoors and all hospitality venues will be subject to an indoor curfew of 6pm from Friday until October 25.</p>\n<p>Pubs, bars, restaurants and cafes will be able to continue serving alcohol outdoors up to the existing curfew time of 10pm and hotel restaurants will be allowed to operate beyond 6pm but only for residents and without alcohol.</p>\n<figure class="embed embed--image alignnone" style="max-width: 620px;">\n\t\t\t<div class="embed--image__holder">\n\t\t\t\t<img loading="lazy" class="size-large wp-image-1733974" class="wp-caption-text caption">First Minister Nicola Sturgeon in the Scottish Parliament at Holyrood.</figcaption>\n\t\t</figure>\n<p>Around 3.4 million people living in central Scotland will be subject to even tougher restrictions, with all pubs and restaurants ordered to close entirely until further notice.</p>\n<p>There will, however, be exemptions to the rules &#8211; in all parts of the country &#8211; for services linked to specific life events, such as wedding that are already booked and funerals.</p>\n<p>In a statement to the Scottish Parliament, as daily coronavirus cases in Scotland surged past 1,000 for the first time, the first minister said the &#8220;vast majority&#8221; of pubs and restaurants had worked hard to ensure the safety of their staff and customers.</p>\n<p>But she added: &#8220;Indoor environments, where different households from different age groups can mix, inevitably present a risk of transmission.</p>\n<p>&#8220;That risk can be increased in some hospitality premises if good ventilation is difficult, and if it is hard to control the movement of people. The presence of alcohol can, of course, affect people&#8217;s willingness to physically distance.&#8221;</p>\n<div><div class="flourish-embed" data-src="visualisation/3942424" data-url="https://flo.uri.sh/visualisation/3942424/embed"></div>\n\t\t<script src="//public.flourish.studio/resources/embed.js"></script></div>\n<p>Ms Sturgeon announced new regulations to extend the mandatory use of face coverings in indoor communal settings, including staff canteens and corridors in workplaces.</p>\n<p>And shops and supermarkets will be asked from this weekend to return to two-metre physical distancing and reintroduce one-way systems.</p>\n<p>The announcement was met with despair from Scotland&#8217;s licensed trade, with Stephen Montgomery, spokesman for the Scottish Hospitality Group, warning it could be catastrophic for the sector.</p>\n<p>He said: \xe2\x80\x9cThe first minister has effectively signed a death sentence for many businesses across the Scottish hospitality industry, while the real problem is socialising at home.</p>\n<p>&#8220;We have repeatedly implemented the safety measures required by the government, and more, to protect our customers and staff.</p>\n<p>&#8220;We are part of the solution to combat this virus, not part of the problem. He renewed calls for scientific evidence behind the measures to be published.</p>\n<p>This call was echoed in the chamber by Scottish Labour leader Richard Leonard, who said the sector &#8220;is not Sodom and Gomorrah and should not be treated as such&#8221;.</p>\n<p>&#8220;Why doesn&#8217;t the first minister consider closing down those businesses which are not complying with guidance, instead of shutting down every single business, serving 70% of the population, including those which are fully complying?&#8221; and warned thousands of premises could close for good by Christmas.</p>\n<p><img loading="lazy" class="size-large wp-image-1715389" src="https://wpcluster.dctdigital.com/syndicatenews/wp-content/uploads/sites/54/2020/10/AN-029327_37-1995-06-12-Aberdeen-Grill-Bar-CAJL_32935960-866x564.jpg" alt="" width="840" height="547" /></p>\n<p>Ms Sturgeon acknowledged the new measures would be disruptive and unwelcome to many but pledged support for the firms hardest hit.</p>\n<p>&#8220;I know that the measures we are proposing today will have a significant impact on many businesses,&#8221;
